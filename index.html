About our project

The project wants to develop learning and teaching resources by using innovative tools and methods in STEM education for vocational education students (15-19 years old).

The project aims to create comprehensive learning and teaching resources specifically designed for students aged 15 to 19 who are pursuing vocational education. These resources will focus on STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) subjects and will incorporate cutting-edge tools and innovative teaching methods. By leveraging modern technologies and pedagogical strategies, the project seeks to enhance the educational experience, making STEM learning more engaging, practical, and effective for vocational students. The ultimate goal is to equip these learners with the skills and knowledge needed to excel in their chosen fields and to prepare them for future challenges in a rapidly evolving technological landscape.

The Use of Quadcopters in STEM Education

In Germany, educational programs are conducted where quadcopters are used to teach students the basics of agricultural activities, including field monitoring, resource management, and soil condition analysis. Such activities increase students' interest in agriculture and develop practical skills in agrotechnology.

Atlas

In 2024, the "Atlas of Best Practices in STEM Education" was published, showcasing successful examples of implementing the STEM approach in educational systems of various countries. In particular, Finland has been carrying out an education reform since the 1970s aimed at decentralization and the introduction of innovative pedagogical methods, including the integration of STEM technologies across all areas of the school curriculum.

Creative Bytes Initiative for Rural Areas

The Melbourne-based initiative Creative Bytes offers free STEM education and programming training for rural communities in Australia. The program focuses on developing the skills of teachers in rural areas, enabling them to effectively teach students how to create their own video games. This helps to reduce the educational gap between rural and urban students.
